Abstract

The Project vocabulary defines concepts and properties for describing a project in terms of its stakeholders, concerns, work breakdown, and deliverables. It provides a basis for specialization by domain experts.

4. Concepts

4.1. Authority

An Authority is any entity that asserts a fact in a model. Example Authoritys include Program, Project, and WorkPackage. (The association of an Authority with its asserted facts requires mechanisms outside the vocabulary of OML and OWL proper, e.g., named graphs. Authorization is conveyed by the organization of statements in a repository.)

Subtypes
  • Program
  • Project
  • WorkPackage
Relations
  • authorizes : Authority
  • executes : Process
  • isAuthorizedBy : Authority
  • isResponsibilityOf : Organization
  • produces : Product
  • reliesOn : DevelopedElement
  • supplies : SuppliedElement

4.2. Concern

A Concern is a specific interest that one or more Stakeholders have in the successful completion of a Project or Program and its Missions.
Supertypes
  • base:AggregatedElement
  • base:IdentifiedElement
Subtypes
  • mission:Objective
Relations
  • base:aggregates : Concern
  • base:isAggregatedIn : Concern
  • isRepresentedBy : Stakeholder

4.3. Deliverable

A Deliverable is the result of successful execution of a Process. It may be a prerequisite to another Process.

Supertypes
  • base:IdentifiedElement
Subtypes
  • DevelopmentState
Properties
  • isRequiredNotLaterThan : xsd:dateTime
Relations
  • isDeliveredBy : InterchangePoint
  • isReceivedBy : InterchangePoint
  • participatesIn : Interchange

4.4. DevelopmentState

A DevelopmentState is a kind of Deliverable that represents a defined condition for a DevelopedElement. DevelopmentStates may be unique to the DevelopedElement; example DevelopmentStates for a Product include Preliminary, Draft, and Final.

Supertypes
  • analysis:Characterization
  • Deliverable
Relations
  • analysis:characterizes : DevelopedElement
  • isManfestedBy : DevelopedElement

4.5. Facility

A Facility is any asset (e.g., laboratory, assembly facility, test facility, etc.) whose DevelopmentState may be a Deliverable for a project process.

Supertypes
  • base:IdentifiedElement
  • DevelopedElement

4.6. Interchange

An Interchange represents the intended joint behavior of a pair of Processes. It indicates that a Deliverable may be delivered by one Process and received by the other Process.

Supertypes
  • base:IdentifiedElement
Relations
  • hasParticipant : Deliverable
  • involves : Process
  • involves1 : Process
  • involves2 : Process

4.7. InterchangePoint

An InterchangePoint is a feature of a Process that permits it to participate in an Interchange.

Supertypes
  • base:IdentifiedElement
Relations
  • delivers : Deliverable
  • isInterchangePointFor : Process
  • receives : Deliverable

4.8. Organization

An Organization hasResponsibilityFor zero or more Authoritys.

Supertypes
  • base:IdentifiedElement
Relations
  • hasResponsibilityFor : Authority

4.9. Person

A Person is an individual human being. A Person belongsTo zero or more Organizations.

Supertypes
  • base:IdentifiedElement
Relations
  • belongsTo : Organization
  • hasRole : Role

4.10. Policy

A Policy represents a specific exercise of authority that binds one or more model elements.

Supertypes
  • base:IdentifiedElement
Relations
  • binds : BoundElement

4.11. Process

A Process is an activity authorized by a WorkPackage in support of delivering a Component or producing a Product. Examples include design trajectory , conduct trade study , and review subsystem design .

Supertypes
  • base:IdentifiedElement
  • AssignedElement
  • RealizedElement
Relations
  • interchangesThrough : InterchangePoint
  • invokes : Process
  • isExecutedBy : Authority
  • isInvokedBy : Process
  • isInvolvedIn : Interchange
  • isInvolvedIn1 : Interchange
  • isInvolvedIn2 : Interchange
  • isRealizedBy : Process
  • realizes : Process

4.12. Product

A Product is a document or other artifact supplied by a WorkPackage. Example Products include trade study reports, design documents, and inspection reports.

Supertypes
  • base:AggregatedElement
  • base:IdentifiedElement
  • DevelopedElement
  • SuppliedElement
Relations
  • base:aggregates : Product
  • base:isAggregatedIn : Product
  • describes : DevelopedElement
  • isApprovedBy : Role
  • isConcurredBy : Role
  • isPreparedBy : Role
  • isProducedBy : Authority

4.13. Program

A Program is an Authority that authorizes zero or more Projects.

Supertypes
  • Authority
Relations
  • authorizes : Project

4.14. Project

A Project is an Authority that supplies a related set of Missions in pursuit of a set of Objectives.

Supertypes
  • Authority
  • RealizedElement
Relations
  • authorizes : WorkPackage
  • isAuthorizedBy : Program
  • isRealizedBy : Project
  • realizes : Project
  • supplies : mission:Mission

4.15. Role

A Role corresponds to a set of assignments meant to be filled by a single Person.

Supertypes
  • base:IdentifiedElement
Relations
  • approves : Product
  • concurs : Product
  • delegatesTo : Role
  • hasAssignment : AssignedElement
  • isDelegateOf : Role
  • isRoleOf : Person
  • prepares : Product

4.16. Stakeholder

A Stakeholder is a person or organization with a recognized interest in the successful completion of a Project or Program. Example Stakeholders include scientists, NASA Directorates, nuclear safety advocates, and the general public.

Supertypes
  • base:IdentifiedElement
Relations
  • represents : Concern

4.17. WorkPackage

A WorkPackage is an Authority subordinate to a Project that produces definite Products and/or supplies definite SuppliedElements in the context of that Project.

Supertypes
  • base:Package
  • fse:MassConstrainedElement
  • Authority
  • RealizedElement
Relations
  • authorizes : WorkPackage
  • isRealizedBy : WorkPackage
  • realizes : WorkPackage
  • supplies : mission:Component
